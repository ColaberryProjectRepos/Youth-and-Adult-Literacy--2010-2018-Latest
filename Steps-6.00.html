The analysis indicates that the overall literacy rate is increasing in these regions. Europe and North America have the highest women and youth literacy rates ranging from 98.7% to 99.4%. However, Asian and African countries have the lowest women and adult literacy rates. Average women literacy rate in Asia  and in Africa is  71.1% and 73.3% consecutively. 
Youth literacy rates are generally higher than adult rates. However, African youth literacy rates are lower than the rest of the regions. For example, The average youth literacy rate in Latin America and the Caribbean is 94.5%, compared to 70.9% in Africa.

Addressing low literacy rates is a complex issue that requires cooperation between governments and citizens. To combat this problem, several solutions can be implemented. Here are some suggestions:
  1. Increase access to education: The overall youth literacy rate in these regions is 92%, reflecting a year over year decrease of 0.8%. Therefore, collaboration between governments and NGOs can be a powerful force in promoting education and literacy worldwide. By working together, they can take steps such as building more schools, providing free or low-cost education, and ensuring that every child has equal opportunities to attend school, irrespective of their economic background. Governments have a crucial role to play in providing political stability and enacting policies that prioritize education and literacy promotion, as they often have access to greater resources and the power to enact systemic change.
  2. Promote adult literacy programs: The adult literacy rate in African countries is 65%, with the female literacy rate being 57%. In Asian countries, the overall adult literacy rate stands at 87%, but it has been decreasing by 5.32% year over year. To address the issue of adult illiteracy, governments and NGOs can promote adult literacy programs that provide basic reading and writing skills to adults who missed out on education earlier in life.
  3. Start a community literacy program: The literacy rate of people aged 15 and above in African countries is 69%, while in Asian countries it is 87%. However, in Asian countries, this rate has been decreasing by 4.86% year over year. As a way to contribute to the solution, citizens can take the initiative to start their own community literacy programs, such as a reading club or after-school program. Religious and other social/cultural organizations can play a great role in creating community literacy programs. These programs can help provide additional support for children and adults who are working to improve their literacy skills.
